Why America Needs a Smart Grid
The American power grid is considered one of the world's largest objects yet many parts of it are old and outdated

A lack of sensors and internet connectivity makes the transition to renewable energy creates demand for novel solutions as customers expect power even during days when generating renewable energy is impossible
Currently, generation facilities monitor the consumption of electricity and adjust their demand accordingly
With renewable energy, companies can no longer control when or how much power is generated A smart grid would adjust demand based on generation capacity, reducing the need for excess generation capacity
In America, there are three independent power grids. High voltage transmission lines transmit electricity over long distances and substations distribute electricity to cities and towns.
With a smart grid, it's possible to connect different regions with European style interconnectors and the addition of time of use rates would encourage the development of power storage
When a single fault occurs due to downed power lines and aging infrastructure, utilities need to shut off electricity to entire neighborhoods and send out crews to manually find and repair problems
smart system of disconnects within a neighborhood would automatically isolate problems and notify crews of their location. With protective technologies, specific regions could be isolated and worked on without needing to shut off power to an entire neighborhood.
The breaker box within a home serves as a safety mechanism which prevents homes from using more power than their connection to the grid can support
With smart breaker boxes, high wattage devices could be prevented from operating simultaneously. This allows homeowners to install additional electrical equipment such as water heaters and stoves without upgrading their entire home's electrical system.
Loads within homes consume electricity when turned on and sit idle when not in use
Devices can be designed to harness time of use rates by charging internal batteries during off hours and running off of stored energy during peak hours. Devices could also use a combination of stored power and grid power to reduce their electrical requirements. This results in significant savings for homeowners and means that our devices can consume more energy from renewable sources.
